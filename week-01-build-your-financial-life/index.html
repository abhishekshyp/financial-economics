Human capital ⟶ financial capital: the crossover

Human capital is the present value of your remaining post-tax earnings — it starts high and drains to zero at retirement. Financial capital is what you have saved. The crossover is the day your money starts to outweigh your paycheck.

Income · consumption · saving over the cycle

The residual between the post-tax income line and the consumption line is saving (green) or borrowing (rust). This is the raw material of the Life-Cycle Hypothesis.

Consumption vs permanent income

The dashed line is permanent income — the single smoothed level whose present value equals your lifetime resources. The PIH says an ideal consumer tracks the dashed line, not their volatile paycheck.

Net-worth trajectory — the life-cycle hump

Wealth builds while you work and is drawn down in retirement, producing the characteristic hump. Where it lands at age 85 is your bequest — or your shortfall.

The concepts behind the numbers

Income, consumption, saving & loans

Each year post-tax income splits into consumption and a residual. When consumption exceeds income the residual is negative — that is a loan. Early-career borrowing followed by later saving is exactly the pattern the life-cycle model predicts.

Human capital vs financial capital

A young worker is almost entirely human capital — the discounted value of a career not yet worked. Saving converts fragile, illiquid human capital into durable financial capital. The crossover chart shows the conversion in progress.

Compounding & time value of money

A rupee saved at 23 compounds for ~37 years to retirement; a rupee saved at 55 barely moves. The corpus is the future value Σ Sₜ·(1+r)^(N−1−t). Present value runs the arrow backwards, discounting future rupees at the discount rate d.

Life-Cycle Income Hypothesis (Modigliani)

Rational agents smooth consumption over a lifetime: borrow when young and income is low, save through peak earning years, and dissave in retirement. Aggregate saving is therefore a hump, not a constant — visible directly in the net-worth chart.

Permanent Income Hypothesis (Friedman)

Consumption depends on permanent (long-run average) income, not transitory swings. The permanent-income line is the level annuity whose present value equals lifetime resources; deviations of your actual consumption from it measure how far your plan is from perfect smoothing.

Nominal vs real & the Fisher check

Real values deflate nominal rupees by CPI into today's purchasing power. Present value is invariant to the framing: discounting nominal flows at d equals discounting real flows at the real rate (1+d)/(1+π)−1. If your discount rate barely exceeds inflation, real returns — and real present values — shrink dramatically.
